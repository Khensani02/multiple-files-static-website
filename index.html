Scones are a type of baked good, originating from Scotland, commonly associated with British tea culture. They are small, slightly sweet, and can have a crumbly or soft texture. Typically, scones are made from flour, sugar, baking powder, butter, milk, and sometimes eggs. They can be plain or include add-ins like raisins, currants, or chocolate chips. Often served warm, scones are traditionally accompanied by clotted cream and jam, making them a popular choice for afternoon tea. Variations include savory scones with ingredients like cheese or herbs.
